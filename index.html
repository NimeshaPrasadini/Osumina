About ඔසුමිණ

Osumina is the first Sinhala language support mobile application which enables herbal plant identification without expert knowledge. Further, it recommends illnesses and home remedies for the identified herbal plant and visualizes the spread of herbal plants in Sri Lanka. In addition, Osumina provides a chatbot feature for recommending home remedies to a specific disease and a social media platform for the local community in Sri Lanka. Use of advanced technologies such as deep learning, image processing, natural language processing, automated machine learning and crowdsourcing, Osumina provides the solution for the prevalent problem of accurately identifying herbal plants without expert knowledge.

This Intelligent Herbal Plants Detecting System guides the user on four main problems related to the herbal plants and Ayurvedic medicine sectors. Namely,

  • Identifying Herbal Plants accurately without special knowledge is challenging.
  • Lack of knowledge about home-remedies, diseases and herbal plants outbreak in Sri Lanka.
  • Language Barrier among local community.
  • Herbal Plants related stakeholders require to share their knowledge and experiences with other fellows.

Osumina mobile application covers a broad community as the targeted audience who works in the fields like Ayurvedic medicine, botany, herbology and essentially ordinary native Sinhala people in Sri Lanka. According to statistics, there are nearly 30,000 ayurvedic physicians, 5000 ayurvedic students and additional 10,000 traditional practitioners in the country.

In the future, the team is planning to release the web version of the application, and increase the plant scope which will cover most of the herbal plants in Sri Lanka.

Applicability for real life scenarios

At the very beginning, research team had several field visits around Institute of Indigenous Medicine and Ayurvedic Research Institute, Rajagiriya in Sri Lanka. Through the field visits, the team has obtained necessary knowledge and support to perform the data gathering. Our team has managed to get the support and guidance from the lecturers and staff of the Department of Ayurveda at Institute of Indigenous Medicine, University of Colombo and Ganewatta Medicinal Plant Garden at Kurunegala to get more accurate and reliable data of herbal plants and home remedies.

Additionally, we have already tested our product with the sample of targeted audience and achieved over 90% of accuracies. Therefore, our product is well fit with the stakeholders' requirements who are involved in the Ayurvedic medicine, botany and herbology domains.

Identification of the Herbal Plant Type

Osumina identifies herbal plants based on a photo of a plant part (leaf, fruit, flower) using Image Processing, Deep Learning and CNN(Convolutional Neural Network) based technologies. The main objective of this research component is to accurately identify herbal plants without special knowledge.

Recommendation of Diseases and Home-Remedies for the identified Herbal Plant

After, the application displays the human diseases that can be cured using the identified plant. This research component implemented using AutoML.

GIS-Based Visualization of the Spread of Herbal Plants

The distribution of herbal plants are visualized on a geographical map using GIS-based visualization to overcome the problem of having lack of knowledge about herbal plants outbreak in Sri Lanka among local community.

Sinhala Language support Chatbot

Osumina provides a Sinhala language supported chatbot for recommending home remedies to a given disease. It helps to break the language barrier among local community. This research component developed using Natural Language Processing(NLP) mechanisms.

Sinhala Language support Social Media Platform with Newsfeed Recommendations and Keyword Extraction Features

Provide a Sinhala language supported, crowdsourcing social media platform to herbal plants related stakeholders with the purpose of communicating each other. The personalized newsfeed recommendation developed using machine learning concepts. And the keyword extraction feature is developed using NLP techniques.

3D View Demonstration of Herbal Plants

Additionally, Osumina mobile application has a feature to demonstrate the 3D view of herbal plants. This component developed and trained using photogrammetry Augmented Reality(AR) techniques.
